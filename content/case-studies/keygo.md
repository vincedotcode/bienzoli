## The Challenge

KeyGo was a new car rental business entering a competitive market in Mauritius — competing against established names with decades of brand recognition and international booking platforms like Booking.com and RentalCars.com.

The core challenge: **how do you build trust quickly as a newcomer?** Tourists arriving in Mauritius search for car rental options on mobile. They see multiple providers. KeyGo needed a site that conveyed professionalism, transparency, and local knowledge in the first 5 seconds.

The secondary challenge: **the Mauritius car rental market has specific local friction points.** International tourists want to see familiar payment methods. Local customers care about WhatsApp accessibility. A generic rental site template wouldn't address either audience. KeyGo needed a site built specifically for the Mauritius context.

---

## The Solution

bienzoli built a premium car rental platform that felt established from day one — the kind of site that makes visitors think "this company has been here for years."

The design led with the fleet. Before any pricing or features, visitors see the actual cars — clean photography, specs at a glance, clear visual hierarchy. This is the car rental equivalent of the before/after slider: let the product do the talking.

The local trust layer was critical. The payment methods section showed MCB Juice, my.t money, Emtel Cash, and MauCAS QR alongside international card icons. For a Mauritian customer, seeing those local payment methods is a trust signal: this is a real local business that understands how we pay.

The booking flow was designed to minimize steps to enquiry. No complex booking form. Just fleet browsing → tap to enquire via WhatsApp → conversation starts immediately with pre-filled rental context.

---

## Features Built

- **Fleet gallery with vehicle specs** — clean grid of available vehicles with model, capacity, fuel type, and transmission. Each car card has a "Book this vehicle" WhatsApp link.
- **Mauritian payment method display** — prominent section showing MCB Juice, my.t money, Emtel Cash, MauCAS QR, plus international card logos. Trust-building for both local and tourist audiences.
- **Airport pickup messaging** — dedicated copy section addressing the #1 tourist use case: rental pickup at SSR Airport.
- **Step-by-step booking explainer** — "How it works" section reducing uncertainty in a 3-step visual flow.
- **WhatsApp booking integration** — all CTAs route to WhatsApp with pre-filled booking context (vehicle selected, dates if specified).
- **Testimonial section** — 4 client reviews with star ratings, building social proof for a new business.
- **Mobile-first responsive design** — entire site optimized for mobile-first browsing (the primary device for both tourists and locals in Mauritius).
- **Contact section** — phone, WhatsApp, email, and island pickup/drop-off coverage explanation.

## What Made This Project Distinctive

The payment methods section was the insight that elevated this site above generic car rental templates. The Mauritius payments ecosystem is fragmented — tourists want Visa/Mastercard, locals want MCB Juice or my.t money. No other car rental site in Mauritius displayed this comprehensively. For KeyGo, this became a genuine differentiator visible immediately on the page.

The fleet gallery hierarchy was deliberately simple: photo → name → key specs → CTA. No rental calculator, no date-picker widget, no complexity. For a new business still managing bookings manually, a WhatsApp-first booking flow is more operationally accurate than a form that implies automated availability — and faster to book from the customer's perspective.
