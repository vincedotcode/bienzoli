## The Challenge

Nickel Sew had 20 years of craft experience — repairing upholstery, restoring furniture, tailoring garments — but their entire lead pipeline ran through word-of-mouth and a Facebook page. No website. No searchable presence. No visual proof of their work quality.

The core problem: **their best asset (visual craftsmanship) was invisible to anyone not already in their network.** Potential customers searching "upholstery repair Mauritius" found nothing. New customers had no way to verify quality before reaching out.

The secondary problem: **lead capture was friction-heavy.** Interested customers had to DM Facebook, wait for a response, then continue the conversation via WhatsApp. A well-designed WhatsApp integration could eliminate half those steps.

---

## The Solution

bienzoli built a full business website focused on trust, visual proof, and frictionless contact.

The design philosophy: **show before you tell.** Before any text about services or pricing, the visitor sees before/after restoration comparisons that demonstrate the craft standard immediately. This turns skeptical visitors into warm enquiries without requiring any copywriting to do the heavy lifting.

The contact flow was built around WhatsApp — the real channel where Mauritian business conversations happen. Instead of a standard contact form, every section has WhatsApp deep links with pre-filled messages so the client context arrives in the conversation automatically.

---

## Features Built

- **Before/after image slider** — dual-panel slider showing the same piece before and after restoration. Visceral proof of quality.
- **Service catalogue with quote intent paths** — each service card has a "Get a quote" WhatsApp link pre-filled with the service name.
- **WhatsApp deep links with pre-filled messages** — "Hi, I'd like a quote for [service name]" delivered straight to their WhatsApp.
- **Google Maps integration** — embedded map of the workshop location with directions.
- **Loyalty programme display** — visible loyalty card system that incentivises repeat customers.
- **Sustainability-focused brand messaging** — eco-conscious restoration narrative that resonates with the growing sustainability-aware market.
- **Local payment method trust badges** — MCB Juice, my.t money, bank transfer icons to remove payment friction.
- **Social media integration** — Facebook, Instagram, TikTok links in footer.
- **WhatsApp floating button** — persistent across all scroll positions.

## What Made This Project Distinctive

The before/after slider implementation was the core creative decision. Upholstery and tailoring restoration is a **visual service** — customers make decisions based on evidence, not claims. Rather than writing "20 years of experience, excellent quality," we built a feature that shows the transformation directly. Visitors scroll down and see exactly what they'd be paying for.

The WhatsApp pre-fill pattern is reusable across industries. When a customer taps "Get a quote for chair reupholstery," the WhatsApp message draft already contains their service context. The business owner knows exactly what the enquiry is about before they reply. Lead quality improves, response time decreases.
